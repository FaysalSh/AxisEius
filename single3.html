6 Benefits of Sunset Beach Pilates:

Hereâ€™s why combining pilates with the outdoors can elevate both your physical and mental wellbeing:

1. Natural Ambiance for Relaxation:

Exposure to the natural environment, particularly the beach at sunset, can reduce your stress levels, lower your blood pressure, and improve your overall mood. This is due to a multisensory experience that combines soothing sounds, calming visual stimuli, and a low-impact exercise that strengthens muscles, posture, and mobility. This allows for cortisol levels to drop, putting both the mind and body in a comfortable, relaxed state.

2. Boost of Vitamin D:

Sunlight exposure triggers the synthesis of vitamin D in the skin through UVB radiation. By regulating calcium and phosphorus absorption, Vitamin D is vital for maintaining bone health, supporting the immune function, and improving the mood and mental wellbeing. Additionally, studies have shown that vitamin D is beneficial for the regulation of adipogenic gene expression, meaning that it potentially reduces the genesis of new fat cells in the body and may contribute to weight loss.

3. Enhanced focus and mindfulness:

Awareness of the present moment is important in low impact workouts such as pilates. With mindfulness, the mind and body connection creates a state of balance, relaxation, focus and emotional regulation. The natural environment, particularly the beach and sunset, promotes mindfulness which improves cognitive functions, breathing, blood circulation, and musculoskeletal health.

4. Sunlight regulation of Circadian rhythms:

Exposure to natural sunlight helps regulate your circadian rhythm which governs sleep-wake cycles and the production of hormones. Outdoors pilates can support better sleep, especially when practicing in the morning or early evening. This can potentially support better energy levels and sleep patterns.

5. Hormonal Balance

Outdoor workouts, like Pilates can contribute to hormonal balance in women by decreasing cortisol levels, which reduces stress, and increasing endorphins (aka. The feel-good hormones).

6. Pelvic floor strengthening

Core strength and stability are emphasized in Pilates. This includes pelvic floor muscles that are challenged in several ways. This can aid during pregnancy and postpartum recovery, enhance sexual function by improving sexual sensation and satisfaction, help maintain continence as women age by providing better control over the bladder and bowel movements.
