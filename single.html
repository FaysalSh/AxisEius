Say Goodbye to bumpy skin:

Urea for Keratosis Pilaris

Do you notice bumpy, rough patches on your arms, or thighs? You might have Keratosis Pilaris (or “chicken skin”). But don’t you worry, you have come to the right place. Dermatologists often recommend Urea to help smooth out those bumps.

Keratosis Pilaris (KP) is a harmless condition where keratin clogs hair follicles, resulting in these rough bumps. Harsh scrubbing is not a good solution to get rid of KP, which is why you should avoid using loofas and similar rough tools on those patches.

Instead, you should consider substances like Urea, which allows for more hydration and gentle exfoliation of the skin, it is a humectant which locks in the moisture, and helps break down keratin. It can also aid in reducing redness.

But be careful! It is important to choose the right product.

Here at Axis Eius, we want you to get what you truly deserve, and most importantly be knowledgeable about what you’re buying.

- Creams or lotions should have a 10-20% urea concentration (consult your dermatologist for higher concentrations)

- For noticeable improvements, you should be consistent with the usage of urea-based creams or lotions. Daily use is recommended.

- Lactic (AHA) or salicylic (BHA) acid can be paired with urea to enhance the effects or urea.

Keratosis Pilaris can be a nuisance, but with patience, and the right treatment, you can achieve your desired results, softer, smoother, bump-free skin.
