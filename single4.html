Busting Burnout:

How to spot it, stop it, and thrive again

Ever felt like giving up? Running on empty? You are not alone.

Burnout is the Chameleon of stress. It sneaks up on us amidst our supercharged lives. A shape-shifter that gets even the best of us.

Here at Axis Eius, we help you recognize the signs, shake off the stress, and feel fabulous again!

Burnout can look different on everyone. Whether you are an overachiever always after perfection, a multitasker juggling a million things at once, or an overthinker always worried about what is next, burnout can get to you in many ways.

Moms, caregivers, and even friends can sometimes be left emotionally drained from the people they care about the most.

If you’re clocking in endless hours at work, and cannot seem to detach from your corporate life, you might get to a point of unproductiveness controlled by physical and mental manifestations of burnout: headache, insomnia, cynical thoughts, inability to focus, etc.

We are here to give you tips to beat this silent epidemic of our busy lives:

- Prioritize “you” time

We know it might be hard to unwind, destress and take some time off. We have all skipped a skincare step, and even a skincare night (believe us you don’t want to keep doin that). But setting your mind on what makes you feel good, and giving it a little time out of your busy day, can go a long way. You will thank yourself later.

- Set Boundaries

2 words: Say. No.
Saying no without guilt is the way to protect your energy, and set clear limits whether it’s at work, at home, with family or friends, or with social obligations.

- Stay active

Who says stress-buster, says physical activity.
Get your body moving any chance you can. Dancing, swimming, pilates, or any other sport can help you release endorphins which will boost your mood.
In this article we discuss how you can have fun exercising at home with an exercise ball.

- Nourish your body

While comfort food can be satisfying at times, it can also leave you feeling guilty, bloated, uncomfortable (shocker?! Uncomfortable comfort food. Ironic).
Fuel yourself your nutritious foods that give you energy, adopt a well-balanced diet, and enjoy home cooked meals whenever you can.

- Mindfulness

Simple breathing exercises, meditation, journaling, can all help you calm your mind, and keep your stress at bay.

- Communication

Talk to a friend, family member, partner, or therapist about what you’re feeling or experiencing. They might give you new perspectives, or just lend you an ear to lighten the load.

- Get your Zzz's

Quality sleep is a must!
Creating a calming bed-time routine, avoiding caffeine late during the day, and making your bedroom a sleep sanctuary will completely change the sleeping game.
